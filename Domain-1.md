

**Vulnerabilities** are weaknesses or gaps in an organization's people, processes, or technology that can be exploited, but they only pose a risk when a threat is present to take advantage of them.

**Threats** are potential malicious actors, events, or circumstances — intentional or unintentional — that could exploit vulnerabilities to cause harm or disruption.

**Risk** is the potential for loss or damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability, assessed by evaluating both the likelihood and impact, and effective security management relies on continuously identifying, prioritizing, and mitigating the most critical risks.

**Exposure** An exposure exists when a weakness can be accessed through the environment, aligns with attacker techniques, and lacks sufficient controls to limit impact. Exposure management is concerned with identifying and reducing these conditions continuously.
